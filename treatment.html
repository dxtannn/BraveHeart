SPECIFIC PHOBIA

Phobia of a specific object or situation.

Astraphobia

Astraphobia is the fear of thunder and lightening. It typically affects children, but many adults still deal with a fear of thunderstorms.

Hydrophobia

The word hydrophobia is derived from Latin and literally means "fear of water." For humans, this fear would generally manifest itself as a fear of bodies of water (lakes, oceans, ponds, etc.) or of swimming.

Dendrophobia

Dendrophobia is the fear of trees. Someone with dendrophobia may have extreme fear or anxiety when thinking about or seeing trees. They may stop walking outside or driving just to avoid trees.

Batrachophobia

Batrachophobia is the fear of amphibians, such as frogs, toads, newts, and salamanders. Because amphibians are commonly utilized in childhood pranks, it is not uncommon for people to have had traumatic experiences involving animals like frogs and toads.

Cynophobia

Cynophobia is an extreme fear of dogs. They may have severe anxiety or a full panic attack when hearing a dog bark, watching a movie about a dog or going to a place where a dog might be.

Equinophobia

People who have equinophobia have an extreme fear of horses. They may also be afraid of ponies, donkeys and mules.

Trypanophobia

Trypanophobia is the extreme fear of needles. Specifically, people with this phobia feel afraid of getting injections or blood draws. People with intense needle phobia may delay or avoid necessary medical care.

Dentophobia

Dentophobia is a fear of the dentist. People with this specific phobia feel anxious when they think about going to the dentist or actually visit the dentist. Past negative experiences, family history or feeling a loss of control can lead to dentophobia.

Hemophobia

Hemophobia is an abnormal and persistent fear of blood. It is a subtype of blood, injury, and injection phobia. When a person has this phobia, they dread the sight of blood. This fear often includes their own blood, another person or animalâ€™s, or even an image of blood.

Claustrophobia

Claustrophobia is the irrational fear of confined spaces. People affected by claustrophobia will often go out of their way to avoid confined spaces, such as lifts, tunnels, tube trains and public toilets.

Aerophobia

Aerophobia is an extreme fear of flying. People with aerophobia might feel intense anxiety before or during a flight. This condition can interfere with your ability to travel for work or pleasure.

Glossophobia

Glossophobia or speech anxiety is the fear of public speaking.
